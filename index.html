Rotor report

Design brief

I have been set a task to design a rotor for 4 rotor microdrone specifically for the UK consumer market. I have strict guidelines to follow set out by the stakeholders as I needed to make the design suitable for manufacturability using injection moulding whilst also meeting technical, consumer and environmental needs meaning it need to be consumer and environmentally friendly. whilst also meeting the technical requirements of lightweight, efficient, reliable and able to achieve 15 minutes of flight time and a max speed of 10 metres per second while being suitable for first time drone users.

Introduction

This task will take 15 weeks to complete in this time there should be a planning technique used at the start to outline the projected milestones that have to bet met by the dates set in order to complete the task in the time given to do this I will create a flow chart and a Gant chart. The flow chart to structure how to complete the design and a Gant chart to set the dates each step must be completed by. This will be the first time for me undertaking a task of this magnitude and using this software so it will require a lot of research into UK standards, how to use certain software e.g. CAD and drone specifications e.g. materials used, industry standards, Enviromental impacts and how to meet performance standards set by competing companies.

Specifications

Here is the outline specifications set for this design that require to be met to meet stakeholders need that have been given at the beginning of this task.

Design Objectives:

- Performance Requirements:

The rotor must be lightweight, efficient, and optimised for stability and lift to ensure safe

and reliable flight. It must support a flight time of at least 15 minutes, achieve a maximum

speed of 10 metres per second and provide stable manoeuvrability for novice users.

Additionally, the rotor should be designed to be quiet and contribute to the overall

durability of the drone.

- Manufacturability:

The rotor must be designed for injection moulding, incorporating best practices for mass

production, including mould flow analysis, material selection, and design for assembly. The

material must be suitable for mass production while being cost-effective and durable.

 

 

- Sustainability Considerations:

The material chosen for the rotor should be environmentally friendly, considering

recyclability and a low carbon footprint. The design should aim to minimise material waste,

use durable or biodegradable plastics, and ensure ease of maintenance and repair to

extend product life cycles. This approach aligns with global sustainability trends and caters

to environmentally conscious consumers.

- Regulatory Compliance:

The rotor must meet relevant UK regulations, including compliance with CAA guidelines

and CE marking. The entire drone, including all four rotors, should weigh no more than

250g to comply with UK drone regulations.

- Aesthetic Considerations:

The rotor should feature a modern design that helps the product stand out in the

competitive consumer market. Scalability and adaptability for future enhancements

without significant redesign should also be considered.

Stakeholders:

- Consumers:

The final product should be appealing to tech-savvy consumers in the UK, with safety and

ease of use as major considerations. Features such as rotor guards should be included to

protect users and enhance product durability.

- Manufacturers:

The design must meet manufacturability standards, ensuring cost-effectiveness and

profitability, while being optimised for injection moulding.

Constraints:

- The rotor must be designed for manufacture using the injection moulding process.

- Material selection should be appropriate for the intended use, focusing on durability,

recyclability, and cost-effectiveness.

- The design must comply with relevant UK and EU safety standards for consumer drones.

- The rotor must be compatible with standard brushless DC motors used in microdrones.

By integrating these elements, the rotor will offer an affordable, high-quality, and

sustainable solution for the UK microdrone market, contributing to the success of a product

intended for recreational and educational use.
